Daily Food Chart

Diabetes-friendly Indian meals — designed for acid reflux management, weight loss, and steady blood sugar

Type 2 Diabetes Metformin 500mg + Dapagliflozin 10mg Acid Reflux No Red Meat No Coriander Weight Loss Goal

Core Eating Principles

  • Eat vegetables & protein FIRST, carbs LAST in every meal — reduces glucose spike by 30-40%
  • 6 smaller meals instead of 3 large — prevents reflux AND keeps blood sugar stable
  • Stop eating by 10:30 PM — at least 1.5 hours before sleep for reflux management
  • Drink water between meals, not during — reduces reflux and helps digestion
  • No lying down for 30+ minutes after any meal
  • Dapagliflozin makes you pee more — drink 2.5-3L water daily, spread throughout the day
  • Use mint, cumin, fennel, curry leaves, ajwain for flavour instead of excess chilli (no coriander)

Daily Meal Schedule

8:30AM

Wake-up Drink

Hydrate + gentle metabolism activation

Choose One
Warm water + ½ lemon + pinch of cinnamon
Cinnamon improves insulin sensitivity. Lemon is alkalizing despite being acidic.
Fenugreek (methi) water
Soak 1 tsp methi seeds overnight, drink the water in the morning. Proven to lower fasting glucose.
No tea or coffee on an empty stomach — caffeine relaxes the lower esophageal sphincter and worsens reflux.
9:15AM

Breakfast

Protein-heavy start — sets blood sugar tone for the day

Rotate through the week
Option A: Moong dal chilla (2 medium) + mint chutney
High protein, low GI. Add grated lauki/zucchini into the batter for extra fiber. Use pudina-jeera chutney instead of coriander chutney.
Carbs ~25g Protein ~14g Fiber ~6g
Option B: 2 boiled eggs + 1 small whole wheat toast + ½ avocado
Avocados are cheap and plentiful at Albert Heijn. The fat slows glucose absorption from the toast.
Carbs ~18g Protein ~16g Fat ~15g
Option C: Vegetable oats upma (½ cup steel-cut oats)
Cook with onion, beans, carrot, curry leaves, mustard seeds. Steel-cut oats have much lower GI than instant oats. Add a boiled egg on the side for protein.
Carbs ~28g Protein ~12g Fiber ~5g
Option D: Besan (chickpea flour) omelette + sautéed spinach
Whisk besan with turmeric, ajwain, chopped onion, green chilli. High protein, no gluten. Spinach from any Dutch supermarket works perfectly.
Carbs ~20g Protein ~15g Fiber ~5g
Take Metformin 500mg IN THE MIDDLE of this meal — not before, not after. Sandwich it between bites to reduce GI side effects.
11:30AM

Mid-Morning Snack

Prevent blood sugar dip + manage reflux by keeping stomach from being empty too long

Choose One
Handful of mixed nuts (15-20 pieces)
Almonds + walnuts + pumpkin seeds. Buy mixed nuts at AH or Jumbo. Almost zero glucose spike, good fats, keeps you full.
1 small apple or pear with 1 tbsp peanut butter
The fat and protein in peanut butter blunts the fruit sugar spike. Dutch peanut butter (pindakaas) — get the "100% pinda" version without added sugar.
Masala buttermilk (chaas) — 1 glass
Blend yogurt + water + roasted cumin + black salt + mint. Probiotic, cooling, excellent for reflux. No coriander.
Tea/coffee (if desired) — NOW is the time
Black tea or coffee with a splash of milk. No sugar — use a tiny bit of stevia if needed. Having it with/after the snack protects the stomach.
1:30PM

Lunch — Main Meal

Largest meal of the day (but still controlled) — eaten early enough to digest well

Structure: Vegetables first → Protein → Carbs last
Option A: Indian Thali Style
• 1 katori sabzi (lauki, turai, bhindi, baingan — avoid tomato-heavy gravies for reflux)
• 1 katori dal (moong, masoor, or toor — not chana dal, it's harder to digest)
• 1.5 small whole wheat roti (not 3-4!)
• Side salad: cucumber + carrot + radish with lemon + cumin dressing
• Small bowl of plain dahi
Carbs ~45g Protein ~18g Fiber ~10g
Option B: Chicken/Fish Day
• 120g grilled/baked chicken breast or salmon/mackerel
• Use jeera, haldi, black pepper, garlic, lemon marinade (no heavy cream sauces)
• 1 roti OR ⅓ cup brown rice
• Large portion of sautéed vegetables (broccoli, zucchini, bell pepper)
• Raita with cucumber and roasted cumin
Carbs ~35g Protein ~30g Fat ~12g
Option C: Bowl Style
• Base: mixed greens or shredded cabbage
• Protein: grilled chicken / baked fish / paneer cubes / boiled chickpeas
• Carb: ⅓ cup quinoa or brown rice
• Top: roasted seeds, olive oil + lemon dressing, pickled onions
• Side: cup of dal soup
Carbs ~35g Protein ~25g Fiber ~8g
Keep roti/rice portions small. Large carb loads slow gastric emptying and increase reflux. If still hungry, eat more sabzi/salad instead of another roti.
If testing blood sugar: check at 1hr and 2hr after the FIRST bite. Target: under 180 at 1hr, under 140 at 2hr.
5:00PM

Evening Snack

Bridge the gap to dinner — prevents overeating at night

Choose One
Roasted chana (chickpeas) — 2 tbsp
High protein, crunchy, satisfying. Available at any Indian/Toko store in Almere. Add chaat masala (without amchur if reflux is bad).
Sprouts chaat
Boiled moong sprouts + chopped onion + cucumber + lemon + cumin + chaat masala. High protein, high fiber, very filling.
Veggie sticks with hummus
Carrot, cucumber, bell pepper sticks with 2-3 tbsp hummus. Hummus is everywhere in Dutch supermarkets and is excellent — chickpea protein + healthy fat.
1 small banana (slightly green) with 5-6 almonds
Slightly green banana = more resistant starch, lower sugar. Almonds slow the glucose absorption.
This is a good time for a second tea/coffee if he wants one. Avoid on an empty stomach — have it with or right after the snack.
8:00PM

Dinner — Light Meal

Lighter than lunch — body needs less fuel, and late heavy meals worsen both reflux and morning glucose

Structure: Protein + vegetables dominant, minimal carbs
Option A: Khichdi night
• ¼ cup rice + ¼ cup moong dal khichdi (1:1 ratio, so protein matches carbs)
• Generous haldi, jeera, ghee tadka (1 tsp ghee is fine)
• Side: bottle gourd (lauki) raita or plain dahi
• Easy to digest, gentle on stomach — perfect for reflux nights
Carbs ~30g Protein ~12g Fiber ~4g
Option B: Soup + roti
• Big bowl of dal soup or chicken-vegetable soup (homemade, no cream)
• 1 small roti
• Soups are excellent for reflux — warm, liquid, easy to digest
Carbs ~28g Protein ~15g
Option C: Grilled fish + vegetables
• Grilled salmon or pangasius (widely available at AH, very affordable)
• Large portion roasted/steamed vegetables (broccoli, courgette, beans)
• Skip the roti entirely — the protein and fat will keep him satisfied
Carbs ~10g Protein ~28g Fat ~14g
Option D: Paneer/tofu stir-fry
• 100g paneer or firm tofu with bell peppers, mushrooms, onion
• Season with cumin, turmeric, black pepper, a pinch of garam masala
• 1 small roti or skip if not too hungry
Carbs ~15g Protein ~22g Fat ~16g
Dinner must finish by 10:30 PM at the latest. Elevate the head of the bed slightly (15-20°) — this single change reduces nighttime reflux dramatically.
Do NOT take medication with dinner. Take Metformin 500mg + Dapagliflozin 10mg at 10:30 PM with the bedtime snack (warm milk or a few almonds). This timing gives better overnight liver glucose control — metformin peaks around 3-6 AM when the liver is most active.
10:30PM

Before-Bed Snack (Optional)

Only if hungry — prevents overnight hypoglycemia and morning reflux from empty stomach

Choose One (keep it tiny)
Small glass of warm milk with a pinch of turmeric
Haldi doodh — anti-inflammatory, calming, and the protein/fat in milk won't spike blood sugar. No sugar added.
5-6 almonds or walnuts
Protein + fat, zero glucose impact. Walnuts also support sleep quality due to melatonin content.
Absolutely no citrus, tomato, mint tea, or chocolate at this hour. Sit upright for at least 20 minutes after this before lying down.

Managing Spicy Food Cravings

Spicy Without the Burn

  • Use black pepper generously — gives heat but is actually GOOD for reflux (improves digestive enzyme production)
  • Ajwain (carom seeds) — adds a sharp, warm flavour and is a natural antacid
  • Cumin (jeera) heavy dishes — roasted cumin gives a warm, earthy depth that satisfies the craving for bold flavour
  • Mustard seeds + curry leaves tadka — big flavour, zero reflux trigger
  • Ginger — anti-inflammatory and actually helps reflux (unlike chilli which worsens it)
  • Small amounts of green chilli are better than red chilli powder — if he must have chilli, fresh green chilli in small quantity is less irritating than dried red chilli powder
  • Avoid combining spicy + oily + acidic — it's the combination that really triggers reflux, not mild spice alone

Foods to Avoid or Strictly Limit

Diabetes reasons White rice (or limit to ⅓ cup), maida products, fruit juices, sugary drinks, potatoes in large amounts, white bread, sweetened yogurt
Reflux reasons Tomato-based gravies, raw onion in excess, citrus on empty stomach, carbonated drinks, deep-fried foods, mint tea (relaxes LES), chocolate, very spicy curries
Weight loss reasons Ghee beyond 1 tsp/meal, fried snacks (samosa, pakora, chips), multiple rotis, rice + roti in same meal, sweetened chai, biscuits with tea
Combination danger foods Biryani (high carb + high fat + delayed spike), naan/kulcha (maida + butter), chole bhature, sweet lassi, mithai/Indian sweets of any kind

Hydration Schedule (Important with Dapagliflozin)

  • 8:30 AM — 1 glass warm water (wake-up drink)
  • 10:30 AM — 1-2 glasses water (between breakfast and snack)
  • 12:30 PM — 1 glass water (30 min before lunch)
  • 3:00 PM — 1-2 glasses water
  • 4:30 PM — 1 glass water (before evening snack)
  • 7:00 PM — 1 glass water (30 min before dinner)
  • 9:30 PM — small sips only (too much water late = nighttime bathroom trips + reflux)
  • Total target: 2.5-3 litres/day — Dapagliflozin pulls water through kidneys, dehydration is a real risk

Portion sizes and food choices should be adjusted based on blood sugar readings, weight progress, and how the body responds. Please share this chart with his doctor for review.

The single most impactful habit: A 15-minute walk after lunch and dinner. This alone can reduce post-meal glucose spikes by 30-50%. Even slow walking works.
